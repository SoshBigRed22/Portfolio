As a kid, I was never really able to experience the ocean due to living in an area surrounded by land with no ocean in sight. That was until about 2 years ago when I went to Florida with my parents and my cousin for a small vacation. While there, we had gone to the beach, which is where I got my first experience with the ocean. It was pretty fun, albeit not as fun as it could have been if I knew how to swim, but I did what I could. I got to:

  • find a lot of seashells,
  • see a pelican for the first time,
  • feel sand beneath my feet for the first time,
  • and overall just get a good first experience with the ocean!

Now having the ability to swim, I could likely enjoy the ocean a lot more, go out further into the water, and find out more cool things about the ocean, if I ever get the chance to go to an area near the ocean, that is. Outside of my personal experience, there are a lot of cool things about the ocean and aquatic life that I wish to have the chance to experience one day.

At the same time, however, there are some parts of the ocean I would prefer to avoid. Areas of the ocean where the water is deep, considering that even though I can swim, I can't really swim very well or for too long, I would like to steer clear from. I don't really have a fear of deep bodies of water, better known as thalassophobia, like my mom does, but I do have a fear of the dark, heights, and spiders, which translates pretty well into the ocean with how far from the sea bed one truly is from the surface, how dark the ocean is without gear, and what kind of creatures lurk in the seas. Besides my irrational fears, I actually quite enjoy the sea and its creatures, mostly. Some of my favorite parts of the deep blue sea include some of the habitats, like coral reefs, seaweed forests, and even just the shores of beaches.
