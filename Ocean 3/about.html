Thalassophobia:

The persistent and irrational fear of large, deep bodies of water, such as lakes and oceans, often causing feelings of anxiousness that stem from the distance from land, the vast, unexplored darkness, and the great unknown of what lives deep below our seas.

My Experience with the ocean:

My personal experience with the ocean, albeit limited, is mostly positive considering the fact I have only ever been to the ocean once before, and it was when I did not know how to swim, so I didn't really go far into the water, and instead just remained near the shore collecting sea shells with my parents while observing the surrounding area.

My Fears of the ocean:

While my personal experience with the ocean is relatively positive, I still fear the ocean quite a bit, as does my mom. Not only the fact that about 27% of the seabed across the global ocean has been explored, which might seem decent, only 0.001% of the deep seafloor has been explored, which means we as humans have only truly explored a fraction of the ocean. Along with that, I already have a fear of the dark, or rather, what lies in the dark, and the ocean is incredibly dark, especially with the deeper you go, so I cannot even imagine what is still hidin within its depths. Along with that, while I can swim, I can't swim very well, especially if my life was on the line, so if I were to get stranded at sea, I would likely be done for.
